# Helios Vision — Vision Document

## Purpose

Helios Vision is a privacy-first, on-device AI assistant designed to help visually impaired users understand and interact with their surroundings using commodity Android smartphones.

The system is designed to run fully offline, without relying on cloud services.

---

## Core Idea

Most AI assistance systems today depend on cloud processing.

Helios takes the opposite approach:

- All intelligence runs on-device
- User data never leaves the phone
- Latency is minimized by eliminating network dependency
- Reliability improves in low-connectivity environments

---

## Target Users

Primary:
- Visually impaired individuals in everyday environments

Secondary:
- Developers exploring on-device AI systems
- Researchers in edge AI and mobile inference systems

---

## Key Principles

### 1. Privacy First
No image, audio, or sensor data is sent to external servers.

### 2. On-Device Intelligence
All perception and reasoning runs locally on the device.

### 3. Practical Utility
Focus is on real-world usability, not research benchmarks.

### 4. Incremental Intelligence
Capabilities are added step-by-step, not as a monolithic AI system.

---

## Long-Term Vision

Helios evolves into a general-purpose assistive intelligence layer for mobile devices that can:

- Understand visual scenes
- Describe environments in real time
- Assist navigation and daily tasks
- Work entirely offline

---

## Non-Goals

- Cloud-based AI assistant
- Social chatbot
- Entertainment-focused AI system
